Summary: Is research data looked for in:

Some conclusions

1. The 'landscape' and 'ecology' of data is really, really complex

2. A successful data search service must be extremely comprehensive

3. Data in papers still dominates in life science and most other subjects, but there are exceptions

4. Awareness of repositories is generally low, but there are exceptions

5. Hardly any body has heard of Figshare. Nobody has used it to find data

6. Lots of people would really like to be able to see negative data

7. People assess data in papers in two distinct ways (1. data then paper, 2. paper then data)

8. Reasons for not sharing data abound, but the desire to find data is strong

9. Bringing together lots of sources of data elicited both positive and negative reactions (as well as some blank looks)

10. The complexity is daunting but surely also represents opportunities
